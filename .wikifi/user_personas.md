### Architects & System Analysts
- **Intent:** Understand system functionality, architectural relationships, and business intent without referencing original code; conduct architectural audits; plan microservice or platform transitions.
- **Needs:** Technology-agnostic domain wikis, high-level interaction diagrams, explicit traceability to source evidence, and decoupled business logic independent of legacy technology dependencies.
- **Pain Points:** Legacy repositories obscure critical business logic and architectural intent; manual documentation is resource-intensive, prone to inconsistency, and rapidly degrades as systems evolve; lack of reliable references independent of technical debt.
- **Usage Patterns:** Review synthesized domain mappings and cross-component relationship flows; validate architectural boundaries and data movement; audit transformation lineage and pipeline health metrics; consume execution summaries for reproducibility verification.
- **Use Cases:** Conducting architectural audits, planning system modernization and replacement workflows, validating microservice boundary demarcation, verifying documentation schema compliance across pipeline versions.

### Migration & Engineering Teams
- **Intent:** Rebuild systems and execute migrations without direct interaction with legacy source code; onboard new practitioners efficiently; maintain deterministic processing environments.
- **Needs:** Deterministic, evidence-based knowledge extraction; behavioral narratives structured as acceptance criteria; fault-tolerant processing outputs; explicit gap declarations for ambiguous or incomplete source material.
- **Pain Points:** Significant friction during migration initiatives; opaque legacy systems; rapid degradation of existing documentation efforts; difficulty separating business rules from implementation-specific code; risk of speculative inference during reverse-engineering.
- **Usage Patterns:** Trigger deterministic, stage-gated pipeline executions; review immutable extraction notes and aggregation structures; consume behavioral narratives for reimplementation validation; leverage timestamped audit trails for lineage tracking and fault-tolerant stage resumption.
- **Use Cases:** Independent system reimplementation, microservice migration planning, onboarding engineering staff to legacy domains, verifying documentation reproducibility across identical input states, managing incremental processing and workspace governance.

### Product & Quality Assurance Teams
- **Intent:** Validate system functionality against explicit acceptance criteria; align redesign efforts with actual system consumers and business workflows; ensure feature coverage matches synthesized documentation.
- **Needs:** Scenario-driven behavioral requirements; standardized persona profiles detailing operational goals and usage patterns; technology-agnostic functional responsibility mappings; strict separation of transient artifacts from committed documentation contracts.
- **Pain Points:** Missing standardized reference for validation; reliance on implementation-specific code to infer business rules; difficulty tracing functional responsibilities to verifiable evidence; lack of structured acceptance criteria for legacy systems.
- **Usage Patterns:** Review behavioral narratives and stakeholder persona profiles; map acceptance criteria to extracted domain concepts; verify feature coverage against synthesized documentation sections; utilize validation gates and CI/CD hooks to enforce quality thresholds before documentation sharing.
- **Use Cases:** Functional validation against acceptance criteria, aligning product roadmaps with inferred usage patterns, ensuring redesign efforts match actual consumer workflows, verifying output integrity against predefined domain schemas.

### System Consumers & Stakeholders
- **Intent:** Maintain alignment with operational goals and usage patterns as inferred from code structure; ensure business value is preserved during system transitions; participate in transparent knowledge translation workflows.
- **Needs:** Clear documentation of functional roles, business rules, and interaction flows; transparent mapping of system intent independent of legacy technology constraints; consistent, technology-agnostic terminology across all documentation sections.
- **Pain Points:** Current systems obscure user value within implementation-specific code; lack of transparent documentation regarding actual workflows and operational boundaries; difficulty assessing impact of technical debt on end-user experiences.
- **Usage Patterns:** Reference persona profiles to understand operational goals; review behavioral narratives to validate expected system interactions; participate in stakeholder reviews of synthesized documentation; monitor pipeline observability markers for progress and anomaly detection.
- **Use Cases:** Guiding stakeholder communication during transition phases, validating workflow assumptions against extracted evidence, supporting continuous documentation updates through audit trails, ensuring backward compatibility across pipeline iterations.
